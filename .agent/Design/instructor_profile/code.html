Biography

Dr. Rostova directs the Interactive Systems Lab and focuses her research on how novel interfaces can enhance human cognitive capabilities during complex tasks. With over 15 years of academic and industry experience, she bridges the gap between theoretical models of interaction and practical, deployable systems.

Before joining Project Polaris University, she was a principal researcher at a major tech firm, leading teams in augmented reality interface design. Her work has been published in top-tier HCI conferences including CHI, UIST, and CSCW.
